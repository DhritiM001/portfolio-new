Modern Data Management

Project Summary

Objective: Retrieve, analyze, and synthesize insights from large datasets (Airbnb, Bikeshare, US Demographics) using SQL and data storytelling techniques.


Role: Business Analyst - responsible for metric design, SQL logic, data interpretation, and visual storytelling.


Skills Used: SQL (joins, subqueries, temp tables), metric development, percentiles, audience-specific communication


#1 Airbnb Market Affordability: Where Can Travelers Save Most?

Business Question: Which U.S. metro area offers the most affordable Airbnb rentals based on a composite affordability score?


What We Did:

1. Designed a weighted Affordability Score combining 6 key factors: adjusted price, availability, price per person, price per bedroom, cost efficiency (reviews/price), and minimum nights

2. Retrieved and aggregated data using SQL joins between Listing, Calendar, and MetroArea

3. Focused on shared and private rooms for budget-conscious travelers



Key Insights:

1. Shared rooms scoring 23.22

2. Private rooms scoring 48.01


This was driven by low nightly prices, decent availability, and strong review-to-price ratios.


Limitation: Local cost of living and minimum night requirements may shift traveler preferences, despite affordability metrics.


#2 Bikeshare: Which City’s Rentals Suffer Most from Bad Weather?

Business Question: Do bike rentals drop more sharply in Portland or Los Angeles under poor weather conditions?


What We Did:

1. Focused on 2017 data to normalize comparisons

2. Created weather categories: Clear Skies, Cloud Cover, Precipitation, Low Visibility

3. Compared average daily rentals per weather category using SQL temp tables and joins

4. Normalized each condition by rental averages on clear-sky days


Business Question: Which U.S. metro area offers the most affordable Airbnb rentals based on a composite affordability score?


Key Insights:

1. Portland's bike usage dropped more significantly under cloudy and foggy conditions

2. Both cities saw major rental declines during precipitation

3. Los Angeles rentals were more resilient to moderate weather changes


This suggests Portland's bikeshare business is more sensitive to weather, likely due to cultural and infrastructure differences.


#3 Population Density & Growth: What Drives County-Level Shifts in PA?

Business Question: Are denser counties in Pennsylvania growing faster than sparsely populated ones?


What We Did:

1. Classified all counties into 3 tiers using population density percentiles (from 2011 data)

2. Calculated % population change (2011–2021) for each county

3. Grouped and averaged change by density category



Key Insights:

1. Densely populated counties grew by an average of +3.25%

2. Moderate-density counties shrank by -1.78%

3. Sparsely populated counties declined even further by -4.85%


This shows a strong urbanization trend and concentration of growth in population-dense areas.


Implication: This insight could inform infrastructure planning, healthcare resource allocation, or real estate investment strategy.
